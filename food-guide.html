Accra Food Guide

Accra's food scene is one of its most honest and exciting entry points. From 22-year-old institutions in Osu to a legendary street stall that runs out of food by noon — here's where to eat, and who each spot is best for.

Buka Restaurant

Best for: First-Time Visitors
10th Lane, Oxford Street, Osu, Accra

Buka is the unofficial first stop for anyone being introduced to Ghanaian cuisine. Open since 2003, it has quietly outlasted every trend in Accra's food scene and remains the go-to recommendation for a reason: consistent quality, a wide menu, and a two-story tree-house setting that makes the meal feel like an occasion. The menu spans Ghana and West Africa — fufu and light soup, groundnut soup, banku, jollof rice, fried kelewele — and daily rotating specials keep regulars coming back. It's upscale enough to feel comfortable for visitors unfamiliar with chop bar culture, but the food is completely genuine. Open Monday–Saturday, 12pm–9pm.

Asanka Local

Best for: Foodies & Culture Seekers
Near Papaye, Oxford Street, Osu, Accra

Asanka Local is always busy, which tells you everything. This is the most recommended local food restaurant in Accra for visitors who want to eat the way Accra actually eats — no concessions, no tourist-proofing, just proper Ghanaian food served in a lively canteen atmosphere. The tomato-rich jollof has perfectly separated grains; the banku-and-tilapia is grilled to order and eaten with your hands. High turnover means the pots are always fresh. Order the jollof with grilled chicken and fried plantain, or go straight for the banku and tilapia and a cold Malta. You will not be disappointed.

Auntie Muni's Waakye

Best for: Street Food Adventurers
Ridge, Accra (near Kofi Otieku Street)

Waakye — rice and black-eyed beans slow-cooked together, typically served at breakfast — is one of Ghana's great street foods, and Auntie Muni's is the most famous place to eat it in Accra. Locals have been lining up here for years. A full plate arrives loaded with accompaniments: spaghetti, fried fish or meat, boiled egg, avocado, shito (black pepper sauce), and gari (cassava flour) sprinkled on top. It's a lot, in the best possible way. Go early — by noon the best portions are gone. Cash only, eat standing or nearby. This is the real Accra.
