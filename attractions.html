Top Attractions in Accra

Accra is a city that rewards the curious. These are the places that define it — from the monuments of Ghana's hard-won independence to the ancient fishing neighborhoods and the beaches the city unwinds on every weekend.

Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park

Nkrumah Avenue, Accra Central

This park and mausoleum is the spiritual center of modern Ghana. It honors Kwame Nkrumah — Ghana's first president, visionary Pan-Africanist, and the man who led the country to independence from British colonial rule in 1957. The grounds are serene and well-kept, with a striking black and white marble mausoleum, a museum tracing Nkrumah's life, and a statue that feels like a declaration. Even if you know little about Nkrumah before you arrive, you'll leave understanding why he matters — not just to Ghana, but to the entire African continent.

Jamestown

Jamestown, Accra Central

Jamestown is the oldest neighborhood in Accra — a dense, charismatic fishing community built around a colonial-era lighthouse that you can still climb for sweeping views of the coast and the city behind it. The streets are alive with color: brightly painted fishing boats pulled onto the beach, murals covering crumbling walls, children playing in alleys between colonial buildings. This is the Accra that tourists often rush past. Don't. Walk slowly, talk to people, and let the neighborhood show you what it is. The boxing gym here — the Attukwei Art Foundation — is also one of Accra's most interesting creative spaces.

Labadi Beach

La Pleasure Beach, Labadi, Accra

Labadi Beach — officially La Pleasure Beach — is where Accra exhales. On weekends it transforms into the city's biggest open-air party: live highlife and Afrobeats music, grilled tilapia straight from beachside vendors, horses to ride along the surf, and a crowd that is effortlessly, magnificently dressed. It has an admission fee and is well-maintained compared to other beaches in the area. Go on a Saturday afternoon if you want to see Accra at its most joyful. Go on a weekday morning if you want the sand mostly to yourself.

Independence Square

High Street, Accra Central

Also known as Black Star Square, this is one of the largest city squares in the world and the symbolic stage of Ghanaian national life. The Independence Arch stands at its center — a bold structure inscribed with "Freedom and Justice," the words Nkrumah spoke when Ghana became the first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence in 1957. The Black Star Monument nearby honors those who died in the fight for freedom. It's grand and open and feels like standing inside a country's sense of itself. Come at sunset when the light hits the arch and the ocean breeze rolls in from behind it.
